Definition

Eugenics is the science of controlling human mating to create a population with desired traits.

There are two types of eugenics:

  • Positive eugenics
    Encourages people with desired traits to mate
  • Negative eugenics
    Kills or sterilizes people with undesired traits

Origins

  • Plato suggested that the state should control mating to create a strong guardian class in his book Republic 1
  • Sir Francis Galton is the father of modern eugenics, coining the term in his book Inquiries into Human Faculty and Its Development
  • Galton made major contributions to the field of genetics, including a basic form of twin studies - the primary tool of behavioral genetics 2

"Downfall" of Eugenics

After World War II, eugenics fell out of public favor. In the decades following the war, many eugenics organizations rebranded and denounced their past

  • The Eugenics Education Society became the Galton Institute
  • The American Eugenics Society became Society for Biodemography and Social Biology
  • The Eugenics Records Office shut down

Eugenics Today

Today, the research that used to be considered eugenics has now been dispersed to many fields of genetics.
The most important fields are:

  • Gene therapy
    Treating diseases with genetic causes
  • Pre-implantation genetic diagnosis
    Genetic screening of zygotes before implantation
  • Behavioral genetics
    Study of genes' relationship to behavior, intelligence, mental illness

The Bell Curve

The Bell Curve by Richard J. Herrnstein and Charles Murray is a controversial book that argued that intelligence is not influenced by education/SES and followed by statistics showing that American Blacks have a lower IQ than American Whites (implying a genetic difference)

Criticisms:

  • Motivation is racism - cited racist literature
  • Devlin et al. - education affects IQ more than estimated in The Bell Curve
  • APA agreed with findings, but not premise nor genetic explanation

The Pioneer Fund

The Pioneer Fund is a non-profit organization that funds scientific racism. It was founded by members of many eugenics organizations.

  • Funds white-nationalist journal Mankind Weekly's publisher Roger Pearson
    Many of the authors cited in The Bell Curve have been published in Mankind Weekly 1
  • Richard Lynn, who serves on the board of The Pioneer Fund, recently authored Eugenics: A Reevaluation where he argues that eugenics is good and that new genetic advances are eugenics
